The Trap

You asked at 5pm Friday. Kids were loud. Dinner wasn't sorted. She was already problem-solving three things. M was zero — she felt informed, not considered. A was zero — she'd have to scramble. P was the worst possible window. You pulled zero levers, then wondered why the answer was "sure, but..." Multiply anything by zero and you get zero. That's the formula working against you.

The Fix
B = M × A × P. If any lever is zero, the behavior doesn't fire. Set all three before the ask. The ask is the last thing, not the first.

Your Move
Sort the kids before Tuesday. Organise dinner for Friday. Tell her Saturday morning is hers — that's M set. Now A: kids covered, dinner handled, her morning locked in before the ask exists. Then find the window. Not 5pm Friday when she's drowning. When she's got space. Walk in and say: "Hey — kids are sorted Friday. Dinner's organised. Saturday morning is yours. Was thinking of catching up with the boys." She doesn't have to think. You already removed every objection before she could form one. That's all three levers. That's the yes.
