The Trap

When four things land at once, the instinct is to deal with the loudest one first. The boss message feels urgent — so it gets your attention. But it said it wasn't urgent. Meanwhile the dinner order has a closing window and the smallest job on the list. Two taps on an app. Done. While you were composing a reply, that window shut. Now everyone eats at 8pm.

The Fix
(Cost of Delay + Time Criticality) ÷ Job Size. Highest score goes first.

Your Move
Pickup goes first — the window is now. Order dinner on your phone while you walk to the car. Two taps. That closes a 8pm problem before it starts. Sports logistics sort themselves in the car. The boss message replies when you're parked. Start with the one that expires. Queue the rest. The freeze goes away the moment you have a sequence.
