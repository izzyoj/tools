You don't have two decisions to make.
You have a sequencing problem.

Sequencing Thinking

→
"Which costs more to delay?"
"Compare salary and title"
→
"What's the job size of each?"
"Wait and think it through"
→
"Time criticality scores it for you"
"Make the big decision first"
→
"Shortest job, highest cost — goes first"
The Trap

When two options exist, the instinct is to make the big decision first. So you deliberate, compare, delay. Meanwhile one option had a tiny job size and a closing window. While you were thinking, that window shut. You made no decision at all and lost the one you could have taken in an afternoon.

The Fix
Cost of Delay divided by Job Size. Highest score goes first.

Your Move
List your two options. Rate each on Cost of Delay (1-3) and Job Size (1-3). Divide. The higher score goes first. The big decision waits until you have more information.
